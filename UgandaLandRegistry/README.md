# Uganda Land Registry: Property & Spatial Data Administration

## Thematic Area
**Area 5: Land & Property Administration** (Land registration, titling, spatial data management)

## The Problem
Land administration in Uganda has historically been challenged by paper-based record-keeping, slow processing times, overlapping titles, and fraudulent transactions. Citizens and businesses face significant hurdles when trying to verify land ownership or process land transfers. The lack of a transparent, easily accessible digital registry leads to prolonged land disputes and inhibits economic development that relies on secure property rights.

## The Solution
The **Uganda Land Registry** is a digital platform designed to modernize property administration and secure land tenure.

The prototype features:
1. **Public Verification Portal:** A web interface where citizens, banks, and legal entities can enter a plot/block number to verify the current registered owner and check for any existing caveats or encumbrances, reducing fraud.
2. **Administration Dashboard:** A secure backend for Ministry of Lands officials to digitize paper records, process transfer requests, and manage spatial data/maps associated with land titles.
3. **Audit Trail:** A transparent log of all changes made to a land title, ensuring accountability and reducing unauthorized alterations to property records.

This solution demonstrates how digitizing core public records can drastically improve transparency, security, and service delivery for Ugandan citizens.
